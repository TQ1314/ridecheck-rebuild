Three Small Additions That Dramatically Increase Trust
These are high-impact but simple additions used by inspection services, marketplaces, and remote services.

They remove buyer anxiety.

A. Add a “See a Sample Report” Section
Buyers want to know:

“What exactly do I get?”

Add this section right after How RideCheck Works.

Example
See What Your RideCheck Report Looks Like

Every RideCheck inspection is compiled into a clear buyer-ready report.

Your report may include:

• Photos of the vehicle condition
• Mechanical observations
• Diagnostic scan results (when available)
• Visible wear or damage indicators
• Notes that may affect your purchase decision

[View Sample Report]

This increases conversion because people visualize the result.

B. Add a “When RideCheck Is Most Useful” Section
This helps buyers instantly recognize their situation.

Example
RideCheck Is Most Helpful When:

• You're buying from Facebook Marketplace or Craigslist
• The vehicle is in another city
• You want a second set of expert eyes
• You’re unsure what to look for when inspecting a car
• The seller says the car is “perfect”

Don’t rely on seller descriptions alone.

C. Add a “What RideCheck Is Not” Section
This actually builds trust.

Because buyers know you’re being honest.

Example
What RideCheck Is — And What It Is Not

RideCheck provides independent inspection intelligence before you buy.

RideCheck is not:

• A vehicle warranty
• A guarantee of future mechanical performance
• A substitute for a full repair shop diagnostic

Instead, RideCheck helps you make an informed buying decision before money changes hands.

Transparency increases credibility.

2️⃣ Should The Homepage Be Split Into Clickable Sections?
Yes — absolutely.

Modern sites should be scrollable sections, not long text blocks.

Your homepage should be structured like this:

Recommended Homepage Structure
Section 1 — Hero
What it is in 3 seconds.

Do Not Buy Blind. RideCheck It.

4 simple steps.

Concrete inspection items.

Section 5 — When RideCheck Is Useful
Help buyers identify themselves.

Section 6 — Pricing

Section 7 — FAQ
Address objections.

Section 8 — Final Call To Action
Reinforce value.

Important Design Principle
People don’t read websites.

They scan sections.

Each section should answer a question:

Section	Buyer Question
Hero	What is this?
How it works	How does it work?
What gets checked	What do I get?
Sample report	What does the output look like?
When useful	Is this for me?
Pricing	How much does it cost?
FAQ	What could go wrong?
